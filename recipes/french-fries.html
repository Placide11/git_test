French fries

French fries are a beloved and popular side dish that pairs well with burgers, sandwiches, or can be enjoyed on their own. These crispy, golden-brown potato sticks are a delight to the taste buds. Making French fries at home allows you to control the quality of ingredients and customize the seasonings according to your preferences.

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Start by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into evenly sized sticks or wedges.
  2. Rinse the potato sticks under cold water to remove excess starch, then pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large pot over medium-high heat. The oil should be about 350°F (175°C).
  4. Carefully add a batch of potato sticks to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them in batches if necessary.
  5. Fry the potatoes for about 5-6 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  6. Using a slotted spoon or tongs, remove the fries from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  7. Sprinkle salt over the freshly fried French fries while they're still hot.
  8. Repeat the frying process with the remaining potato sticks.
  9. Your homemade French fries are now ready to be enjoyed!
